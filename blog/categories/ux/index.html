Material Design - We Live Only to Discover Beauty

… All else is a form of waiting

This quote by Kahlil Gibran suits the rapid development in design quality we’ve seen in the digital space over the past years, we continue to see and experience really beautiful and contemplated design. At this year’s Google I/O, Google’s design team presented the thing they’ve been spending all their time on, “Material Design”.

It’s been almost three years since Google presented their last UI change, “Holo”, which many of the popular apps on Android has tried to adopt. Android apps used to be extremely fragmented when it came to design quality, but over the years as Holo has existed, more and more apps have that “natural android look and feel”. Most Android apps has moved away from the “no-design and shitty” or “ios-look-a-like” infamous design quality in which the early apps had on the platform. Before this years Google I/O we all got a sneak peek at what we could expect from the design team with the release of the new Google+ app, which drastically changed the look and feel of an Android app which we were so accustomed to.
